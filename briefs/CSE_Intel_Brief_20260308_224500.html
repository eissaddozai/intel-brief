01 BATTLESPACE — KINETIC [high]
What is the current disposition of forces, and what military operations occurred across all theatres in the last 24 hours?
KEY JUDGMENT: We assess with high confidence that the Israeli Air Force has shifted from degradation strikes to systematic destruction of Iran's ballistic missile production and oil refining capacity, with 1,465 munitions dropped on 400+ targets over the 6-7 Mar weekend.
OBSERVED ACTIVITY

As of 1800 UTC 8 Mar, the Israeli Air Force attacked more than 400 targets in Iran over the 6-7 Mar weekend, dropping approximately 1,465 munitions on regime military sites including ballistic missile launchers and weapons production facilities (Alma Research, 8 Mar 1800 UTC). In Tehran alone, more than 80 IAF strikes were confirmed. US-Israeli forces struck five oil storage depots and refining facilities in and around Tehran, cloaking the capital in black smoke that blocked out the sun (France24, 8 Mar; BBC, 8 Mar). Tehran Governor confirmed fuel distribution was 'temporarily interrupted' (France24, 8 Mar). The Iranian Red Crescent warned Tehran residents to avoid noxious fumes from fires at struck oil depots (France24, 8 Mar). Hezbollah continued operations on the northern front; Alma Research assessed thousands of anti-tank missiles remain operational, with the Almas precision missile the most advanced threat to IDF ground forces in southern Lebanon (Alma Research, 8 Mar).

Alma Research (T2), France24 (T2), BBC (T2), Al Jazeera (T1)
OPERATIONAL ASSESSMENT

We assess the coalition campaign has entered a war-sustaining infrastructure destruction phase; the targeting of oil sites in addition to military facilities signals intent to degrade Iran's capacity to fund and fuel protracted operations. The IRGC's public claim that Iran can sustain 'intense war' for six months (France24, 8 Mar) should be read as a morale-sustaining assertion rather than an operational assessment, given the confirmed disruption to fuel distribution in the capital. CTP-ISW assessment flags Houthi and Iranian potential to target Emirati, Israeli, or US positions in the Horn of Africa, indicating the battlespace may extend to the Red Sea and East Africa (CTP-ISW, 5 Mar).

France24 (T2), CTP-ISW (T1)
02 ESCALATION — TRAJECTORY [high]
What is the probability and direction of conflict escalation or de-escalation in the next 24-72 hours?
KEY JUDGMENT: We assess with high confidence that the conflict has entered sustained horizontal expansion; Iran has struck five Gulf Arab states, the Arab League has convened in emergency session, and the Khamenei succession creates unpredictable factional dynamics within Iranian command.
ESCALATION INDICATORS

As of 8 Mar, Iran has struck five Gulf Arab states while simultaneously issuing a presidential apology—a contradiction that indicates fractured command authority between the IRGC and civilian government (France24, 8 Mar). The Arab League has convened an extraordinary session to address Iranian strikes on member states (France24, 8 Mar). Trump and UK Prime Minister Starmer discussed military cooperation amid tensions over Iran strikes, with London's mayor stating the UK premier was 'right to resist Trump pressure to join' the campaign (Anadolu, 8 Mar). France has dispatched fighter jets and warships to defend Gulf partners (RFI, 8 Mar). Analysts warn that the assassination of Khamenei could lead to 'dismembering of the Iranian state' through fragmentation of central authority (Responsible Statecraft, 8 Mar).

France24 (T2), Anadolu (T2), RFI (T2), Responsible Statecraft (T2)
TRAJECTORY ASSESSMENT

We judge it unlikely that meaningful de-escalation will materialise in the next 72 hours. The Khamenei succession introduces a period of maximum uncertainty: Mojtaba Khamenei must establish authority over both the IRGC and civilian government while under sustained military assault. Israel's stated intent to 'pursue every successor' (France24, 8 Mar) removes any incentive for the new leadership to signal willingness to negotiate. The sole de-escalation signal—Pezeshkian's apology to Gulf neighbours—was immediately contradicted by continued Iranian strikes, suggesting the president lacks authority over military operations. Trump's engagement of Kurdish opposition groups to launch attacks inside Iran (Responsible Statecraft, 8 Mar) opens an additional internal front that further reduces prospects for stabilisation.

France24 (T2), Responsible Statecraft (T2)
ANALYST B — DISSENTING VIEW

ANALYST B assesses that Pezeshkian's apology may represent a genuine factional push for off-ramps. If the civilian government can assert authority over the IRGC during the succession transition, a unilateral ceasefire offer within 7-10 days cannot be excluded (probability: 15-25%). This view is not shared by the primary assessment.

03 ENERGY — ECONOMIC [high]
What is the impact on energy markets, shipping routes, and regional economic stability?
KEY JUDGMENT: We judge it highly likely that energy markets have crossed a structural break; Brent crude has breached $100/bbl, Gulf Arab producers are cutting output due to Hormuz export inability, and US-Israeli strikes on five Iranian oil sites have disrupted fuel distribution in Tehran.
ENERGY INDICATORS

As of 8 Mar, Brent crude topped $100/bbl as Gulf Arab producers cut production because they cannot export through the Strait of Hormuz (CNBC, 8 Mar). Traffic through the Strait has nearly come to a standstill after three Indonesian crew members went missing when a UAE-flagged tugboat sank following an explosion (France24, 8 Mar). US-Israeli forces struck five oil storage depots in and around Tehran; satellite imagery shows extensive devastation to port infrastructure (France24, 8 Mar). Oil analysts warned that ongoing Hormuz disruption could sustain prices well above $100/bbl (Hellenic Shipping News, 8 Mar). Oil and gas prices 'rapidly rise as Iran war shows no signs of letting up,' with nearly every country in the Middle East sustaining damage from missiles or drone strikes (Hellenic Shipping News, 8 Mar).

CNBC (T2), France24 (T2), Hellenic Shipping News (T2)
SUPPLY CHAIN DISRUPTION

We assess the conflict has created a dual supply-destruction dynamic: direct strikes on Iranian refining capacity remove production, while Hormuz closure prevents Gulf Arab states from exporting existing reserves. The Trump administration's announcement of a $20 billion reinsurance programme for oil tankers (CNBC, 8 Mar) confirms that private insurance markets have effectively ceased underwriting Hormuz transit, forcing government backstop. Breakbulk shipping in the Gulf faces growing uncertainty, with approximately 5% of global breakbulk cargo at risk alongside the dominant oil and gas flows (Hellenic Shipping News, 8 Mar). Arabian Gulf tanker trips are described as 'extremely profitable for those willing to take the risk' (Hellenic Shipping News, 8 Mar), indicating a risk-premium bifurcation between willing and unwilling operators.

CNBC (T2), Hellenic Shipping News (T2)

What diplomatic initiatives are underway, and how are regional and global actors positioning?
KEY JUDGMENT: We judge it highly likely that the Khamenei assassination and forced succession have eliminated near-term prospects for negotiated de-escalation; Iran's new leadership lacks both the authority and the incentive to engage diplomatically while Israel threatens to target any successor.
STATE POSITIONS

As of 8 Mar, Iran's Assembly of Experts announced Mojtaba Khamenei as new supreme leader under IRGC pressure, days after Iran International first reported the selection (Al Jazeera, 8 Mar; Iran International, 8 Mar). Israel warned it would 'pursue every successor' to the elder Khamenei (France24, 8 Mar). Trump spoke with Kurdish leaders on 4 Mar, urging Iranian Kurdish groups to launch attacks inside Iran while warning Iraqi Kurdish leaders not to obstruct US plans (Responsible Statecraft, 8 Mar). The UK discussed military cooperation with the US, though London's mayor publicly stated the prime minister was 'right to resist Trump pressure to join Iran strikes' (Anadolu, 8 Mar). France deployed fighter jets and warships to protect Gulf partners while also confronting Iranian intelligence networks on French soil (RFI, 8 Mar). The Arab League convened an emergency session over Iranian strikes on five member states (France24, 8 Mar).

Al Jazeera (T1), Iran International (T2), Anadolu (T2), RFI (T2), France24 (T2)
MEDIATION STATUS

We assess all mediation pathways remain closed. Gulf Arab states—previously the only actors positioned to broker de-escalation—are now themselves targets of Iranian strikes and have elevated the matter to the Arab League. The contradiction between Pezeshkian's apology and continued IRGC strikes suggests the civilian government lacks control over military operations, making diplomatic engagement with Tehran structurally unreliable. Washington's engagement of Kurdish separatist groups as a second front indicates the US strategy is regime fragmentation rather than negotiated settlement. Iran International reports internal factional tension between IRGC hardliners who engineered the succession and voices around Pezeshkian who favour diplomatic resolution (Iran International, 8 Mar).

OBSERVED ACTIVITY

As of 8 Mar, Iran's government-imposed internet blackout has extended into its second week; the government cut internet connectivity shortly after the start of US-Israeli attacks on 28 Feb (CNBC, 8 Mar). NetBlocks monitoring confirms the ongoing disruption. CISA has issued new cybersecurity advisories, though specific Iran-related threat indicators were not detailed in available collection (CISA, 8 Mar). The combination of internet blackout, degraded communications, and restricted information flow from Iran means assessment of Iranian cyber operations capability is significantly constrained.

CNBC (T2), CISA (T3)
ASSESSMENT

We assess the prolonged internet blackout serves dual purposes: preventing domestic population from accessing real-time strike damage assessment, and degrading OSINT collection on Iranian military disposition. The blackout itself represents the most significant information operation of the conflict, affecting an estimated 85 million Iranians. Offensive cyber operations by Iranian-affiliated groups against coalition targets likely continue but are below our collection threshold. The absence of major reported cyber incidents against Western infrastructure may indicate Iranian cyber capacity is being redirected to defensive measures or internal communications security.

How are war risk premiums, marine insurance markets, and shipping finance responding to the conflict?
KEY JUDGMENT: Available evidence suggests the maritime war risk insurance market has entered crisis mode; supertanker charter costs have hit all-time highs, at least three tankers have been struck in the Gulf, and the US government's $20 billion reinsurance programme confirms private market capacity exhaustion.

The International Group of P&I Clubs has finalised its Pooling and Group Excess of Loss Reinsurance contract structure for the 2026/27 policy year (IG P&I, 8 Mar).

MarineLink (T2), France24 (T2), UKMTO (T1), IG P&I (T1)
PREMIUM MARKET

We assess the $20 billion US government reinsurance programme for oil tankers (CNBC, 8 Mar) represents a de facto admission that private war risk markets have ceased functioning for Hormuz transit. This is the most significant government intervention in marine war risk since the World War II War Risk Insurance Programme. Arabian Gulf tanker trips are described as 'extremely profitable for those willing to take the risk' (Hellenic Shipping News, 8 Mar), indicating a binary risk environment where operators either accept catastrophic exposure or avoid the region entirely. Dry bulk shipping rates show volatility as the war intensifies (Hellenic Shipping News, 8 Mar), and Hapag-Lloyd's proposed acquisition of an Israeli shipping company has collided with national-security concerns at a particularly volatile moment (Hellenic Shipping News, 8 Mar).

CNBC (T2), Hellenic Shipping News (T2)
