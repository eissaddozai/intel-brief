HEADLINE JUDGMENT

The IRGC's first kinetic enforcement of the Hormuz passage ban — a drone strike on a transiting tanker — combined with Tehran's explicit rejection of ceasefire talks and the IAF's targeting of nuclear laboratories in Tehran, confirms the conflict has crossed into a self-reinforcing escalation cycle where both sides are destroying the other's offramps.
Three convergent indicators point to continued escalation. Ghalibaf declared Tehran 'not seeking a ceasefire' and insisted 'the aggressor must be struck' (Middle East Eye, 10 Mar 0700 UTC). IRGC drone strikes tanker enforcing Hormuz ban
The IRGC struck a tanker with a drone for 'violating' its self-declared Strait of Hormuz passage ban, the first confirmed kinetic enforcement against a commercial vessel. A separate sabotage attack hit a US-managed tanker; crew safe in both incidents (TradeWinds, 9 Mar).
Turkey summons Iran ambassador over airspace violation
Turkey summoned Iran's ambassador after a ballistic missile transited Turkish airspace. FM Fidan warned Tehran that airspace violations are unacceptable, marking the sharpest diplomatic response yet from a non-belligerent NATO state (Middle East Eye, 10 Mar; Reuters via Middle East Eye).
