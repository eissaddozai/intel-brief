HEADLINE JUDGMENT

Evidence strongly indicates that Mojtaba Khamenei's formalisation as supreme leader has consolidated IRGC control over Iran's war strategy, while the insurance market's repricing — LR2 MEG/Japan freight doubling to WS446 in a single week — confirms that commercial actors are pricing sustained conflict rather than a negotiated resolution. The Israeli strike on a central Beirut hotel targeting IRGC commanders signals the campaign has expanded beyond Iranian territory into active decapitation operations across the region.
Three independent escalation signals converged in the past 24 hours. First, Iran's Assembly of Experts formalised Mojtaba Khamenei as supreme leader with IRGC and military pledges of allegiance, completing the wartime succession (Al Jazeera, 9 Mar 0000 UTC). Second, Israel struck a Ramada hotel in central Beirut targeting Iranian Revolutionary Guards commanders, killing at least four and expanding the campaign's geographic scope into civilian infrastructure in a third country (France24, 8 Mar 0440 UTC). Third, China suspended oil product export certificates effective 4 Mar to ensure domestic supply, indicating Beijing assesses the Hormuz disruption as protracted (Hellenic Shipping News, 8 Mar). The primary tripwire is whether Mojtaba Khamenei's IRGC-backed authority translates into escalated retaliatory operations or whether the factional split with President Pezeshkian's conciliatory posture creates operational paralysis. Countervailing signal: the IRGC claims Iran can sustain 'intense war' for six months (RFI, 8 Mar), but this assertion is contradicted by confirmed fuel distribution interruptions in Tehran and China's emergency export suspension.

What is the current disposition of forces, and what military operations occurred across all theatres in the last 24 hours?
KEY JUDGMENT: The coalition campaign has expanded to multi-country decapitation operations; the Beirut hotel strike targeting IRGC commanders, strikes on Iranian jets at Isfahan, and elimination of a senior Iranian official in Tehran confirm systematic leadership targeting across three countries.
IRAN THEATRE

US-Israeli forces continued striking Iranian military and economic infrastructure through 8 Mar. The IAF struck an oil depot in Tehran, cloaking the capital in black smoke; the Iranian Red Crescent warned residents to avoid noxious fumes from fires at struck facilities (France24, 8 Mar 0638 UTC). Strikes on five oil storage depots disrupted fuel distribution in Tehran; Governor Motamedian confirmed the interruption to state media (France24, 8 Mar 1012 UTC). The IAF also struck Iranian jets at Isfahan, destroyed 50 ammunition bunkers, and eliminated a senior Iranian official linked to anti-Israel operations alongside a command building for the Khayyam surveillance satellite (Jerusalem Post, 8 Mar 1009 UTC). Blasts were heard in Jerusalem as air raid sirens sounded across Israel and the occupied West Bank following Iranian missile launches (France24, 7 Mar 1951 UTC).
